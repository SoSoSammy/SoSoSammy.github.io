My Journey with Web Dev

In the summer of 2021 I decided to learn HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to become a web developer and create my own websites. I started learning HTML in the beginning of June, around the fourth, and I completed the w3schools tutorial on HTML about two and a half weeks later, around the twenty-second. While I was learning HTML I put my skills and knowledge to the test, working on v0.1 of my website, SoSoSammy. The first version had pictures, lists, a table, and some navigational links. My code was messy and a little difficult to read, but I was proud of my work and website that I created all on my own. Check it out here: The first version of the SoSoSammy website

The second version of my website I created after I read through the entire HTML tutorial. This time as I was working I focused on writing cleaner code and using HTML elements that make sense with the content of my website. I made my website look cleaner and added in a second page. As I was writing this code, I challenged myself to not use w3schools.com for help, so that way I could explore different resources. Check out my website here: The second version of SoSoSammy

Now I am working on v0.3 of my website. I am studying CSS through a tutorial on w3schools. For this website I am continuing focusing on writing better content that flows better and makes sense, along with adding in a nice and neat style to my website. I plan to finish this version and unveil it on July 14th. (It's currently September. I haven't finished this version yet, but I will be done with it by Friday, October 1st!)

My Favorite Books

Since I enjoy reading, I will share with you some of my favorite books. My favorite genre to read in is sci-fi in fiction books. I am starting to read more non-fiction books, and I like reading about personal development and becoming the best version of myself. My favorite books are:

  • Ender's Game by Orson Scott Card
    • This is a sci-fi book that takes place far in the future. An alien species is attacking Earth, and the military is becoming desperate for a strategic commander. This book follows the point of view of six-year-old Ender Wiggin, who is chosen to go to Battle School to train to become a commander. I love this book because of the way it causes you to think deeply, and to see things in ways you had never thought of before.
  • The Alchemist by Paulo Coelho
    • This is a fiction book that takes place in the Eastern part of the world long ago. The story is about a shepherd who travels far in search of his personal legend, something that a person is destined to do or become in his or her lifetime. This book has many wonderful power phrases and quotes that help you to think about life's meaning.
  • The Reckoners series by Brandon Sanderson
    • This is a sci-fi series that takes place on Earth. There was a giant star that appeared in the sky, later named Calamity, that brought with it powers that changed people for the worst. The people who received the powers of Calamity are called Epics. They are greedy and power-driven. David Charleston is a teenager whose father was killed by one of the worst Epics, Steelheart. David Charleston seeks revenge on Steelheart, and joins a group of ordinary people called the Reckoners in an attempt to get even with Steelheart.
